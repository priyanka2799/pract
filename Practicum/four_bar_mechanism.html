FOUR BAR MECHANISM




About the Experiment

A four bar mechanism is a mechanism consisting four rigid links connected by four pin joints in a quadrilateral fashion and a four bar mechanism that obeys Grashof’s criterion is known as Grashof’s four bar mechanism.
Grashof’s criterion: For a planar four bar linkage, the sum of the shortest(s) and longest(l) links has to be lesser than or equal to the sum of the remaining(p,q) links if there is to be continuous relative rotation between two members.




Theory Behind the Experiment

Grashof’s four bar mechanism exhibits multiple behaviors depending on the lengths of the links and the way they are attached.To understand them we fix one of the links and study their behaviors when other links are attached in different combinations,these are called inversions.
Inversions: Every mechanism has links which move relative to each other about the joints connecting them. These relative motions result in the trajectories of the points on links of the mechanism. In any mechanism one link or member is fixed and acts as the frame. The trajectories and motion characteristics of mechanism depend on the choice of the reference frame link,inversions are the different configurations of the mechanism with change of the fixed reference link called frame. By this principle of inversion of a four bar linkage, several useful mechanisms can be obtained.We in general consider three inversions namely,

a) Double Crank mechanism
b) Double Rocker mechanism
c) Parallelogram Linkage


Where, s= shortest link,l= longest link, p,q are other two links.


Procedure

The simulation provided under the Simulation section is user interactive and the user may try various cases and possibilities of the four bar mechanism by changing the lengths of the links as well as the angular velocity of the crank link.
All lengths given are in milimeters(mm) and angular velocity in (rad/s).The lengths and the respective important parameters of the mechanisms can be seen on the top left region of the canvas. For giving lengths such as 119mm, on may click on the slider bar and adjust it using the left and right arrow keys on the keyboard, for precision.
